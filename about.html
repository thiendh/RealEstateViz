Real Estate Market and Home Estimated Value

By Thien Do - 7/29/2018

 

My project shows real estate market trend and home estimated value of a specific property in San Francisco. The property is a 1 bedroom 1.5 bathroom loft in South of Market (SOMA) neighborhood. The presentation data uses MLS data and filtered for 1 bedroom loft sales in SOMA since 2000. It's interesting to see the market trend overall with Dotcom and Real Estate bubbles. 

 

The presentation also shows the estimated home value of the property base on the average sales and average $/sqft. The large gap between sales / selling price and listing price also indicates that 1 bedroom loft is in high demand.

 

Structure & Scene: Interactive Slideshow

Each scene includes an interactive char with annotations and mouseover. User can explore the data within each slideshow. 

There are 3 scenes:

  • Sales Price vs. Listing Price: shows average sales / listing dollar amount for each year from 2000 - 2018 (as of 7/28/18). This gives user an estimate of how much the property valuation is and how much it should be listed for.
    •  
  • $/SQFT: show average sales dollar / sqft and average listing dollar / sqft - similiar trend line with Sales and Listing Price. This chart confirms and creates a range for the property valuation.
    •  
  • Days on Market: Estimate how long the property will stay on the market.
    •  

Annotations: There are 4 annotations on the chart. They point out interesting points with market trend:

  •        Dotcom bubble bust and 9/11 attack - the lowest sales point.
    •    
  •        Real Estate Bubble started around 2005 and busted in 2008.

The other 2 annotations shows the last sale of the property back in 08/2011 at $500,000, and estimated value in 2018 is $930,000.

 

Parameters and triggers: 

Triggers via navigation menu and mouseover. 

Parameter is changed based on scenes. The y axis of 1st chart (dollar amount) shows $ in thousands, 2nd chart ($/sqft) shows $ in hundreds, days on market chart show number of days.
